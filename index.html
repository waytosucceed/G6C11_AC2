Understanding “Adjective vs Adverb”


Convention/Grammar

The adjectives modify and provide additional details about the nouns or pronouns, describing their qualities, characteristics, or states.

On the other hand, the adverbs modify and provide more context about the verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, describing how, when, where, or to what extent an action or characteristic occurs.

Let us take a look at this table to understand “Adjective vs Adverb”:

Adjective Adverb
Describe nouns (people, places, or things) or pronouns Describe verbs (action words), adjectives, or other adverbs
Answer the question: "What kind?" Answer the question: "How?" "When?" "Where?" "To what extent?"
Examples: happy, red, tall: , short, smart Examples: quickly, carefully, gently, patiently, always
Examples in sentences:
  • The happy dog.
  • The shiny ball.
  • The tall tree.
 Examples in sentences:
  • The dog chased quickly.
  • The student wrote carefully.
  • The tree swayed gently.
